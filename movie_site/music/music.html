The Hateful 8

One scene where the music stood out the most to me comes shortly after everyone arrives at Minnie’s. Marquis Warren, Chris Manix, O.B., and Bob are putting the horses away (43:30). In this scene the blizzard is closing in on the characters and the sky has quickly turned a dark gray adding to the unsettling condition the characters are finding themselves in. The chiming still continues as O.B. suggests that they lay out a line in order to get to the out house and the barn safely during the blizzard. Him and Chris grab a handful of metal stakes and make their way back out into the snow as the blizzard rages on. The scene is even darker than it was two minutes ago and as they start to hammer in the stakes high pitched tones start to play in a descending fashion. Upon this more analytical viewing it is clear that the scene and the music is depicting the characters staking their spot in this bad situation. Again the music crescendos as the actions continue and the chimes that were softly playing before are now much louder and more abrasive. The scene is dark and the music is scary. The descending notes continue to play as the characters fall into the trap that was set for them.

Moneyball

The music in Moneyball is rather repetitive with instruments often repeating the same two notes over and over again. It creates moments of tension during games that can go either way, but matches the themes of the movie most prominently when Billy is coaching the team. He even repeats the phrase “it’s a process, it’s a process, it’s a process” right along with the repeating notes of the music. He is talking about change, practice, and exercise. These things are repetitive and tedious and the music reflects this repetitive nature until different notes are introduced and the benefits of that repetitive process come to light and they start to win games.
